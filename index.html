Core Risk Tiers — What Each Level Means
🟢 LOW RISK Standard Approval
  • No access to personal, financial, or sensitive business data
  • Minimal or no integration with internal systems
  • Strong, verifiable security posture (ISO 27001 / SOC 2)
  • Mature policies: encryption, MFA, patch management in place
  • Low business impact if disrupted or breached
  • No regulatory data in scope (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI)
  • Clean incident history — no material breaches
  • Transparent, cooperative responses to TPSQ
🟡 MEDIUM RISK Enhanced Review
  • Access to internal systems but limited sensitive data exposure
  • Security controls present but not independently certified
  • Some regulatory data in scope with partial controls
  • Minor or historical security incidents (resolved with evidence)
  • Business impact is moderate — recoverable within days
  • Subprocessors used but with visibility/controls
  • TPSQ responses are generally good with some gaps
  • Cloud/AI use is acknowledged with partial governance
🔴 HIGH RISK Senior Approval / Reject
  • Access to critical PII, PHI, financial, or confidential IP
  • Deep system integration — admin/privileged access
  • No certifications, self-assessed only or assessments refused
  • Recent material breach, regulatory fine, or litigation
  • High business impact — operations would halt if disrupted
  • Regulatory data with insufficient or absent controls
  • Opacity: evasive, incomplete, or refused TPSQ responses
  • AI/Cloud with data leaving jurisdiction, no controls documented
